 There are many different methods and techniques for the cultivation, mobilization, and use of Qi. ﻿Shaolin includes a number of practices collectively called Neigong. 

Neigong is practice of aligning breath, movement, and awareness as a form of exercise, healing, and meditation.

Throughout there is emphasis on the coordination of movement and breath and often involves soft movements to harmonize inner and outer energy and develop holistic physical and mental well being.​​

The first purpose of Shaolin training is health and longevity. Our older generations has put it to us this way: "Without regular practice you will have nothing in your old age.”

Ian began studying with the Canadian Henan Saolin Wushu Association in 1986, is a senior student of the school, and is certified to teach Lohan Qi Gong and Shaolin Ba Gua.
